MozSetup@IITKGP

To build up a good community to contribute to Mozilla, KOSS (aka IIT Kharagpur Firefox Club) organized it’s first Event ‘MozSetup@IIT KGP’

It was a two day event, with sessions on :
Introduction to Mozilla
Firefox Student Ambassador
Webmaker
Localizer
SuMo
Firefox OS App Development
Bugzilla

The first day had a great participation, it included the introductory sessions.
The session on first day included :-
1. Introduction to FSA and IIT KGP Firefox Club by FSA Devavrat
2. Introduction to Mozilla by Gautham Raj
3. How to be a part of Mozilla and contribute to it – by Saurabh
4. Session on Webmaker by Gautham Raj
5. Session on Localizer by Biraj

And so ended our first event at IIT Kharagpur, which brought opportunity not only to learn a lot but also to meet a lot of new and awesome people. We would like to thank all the Speakers and FSA’s without whose efforts it would have not been possible.

Applied CS with Android workshop

Project Overview

KOSS had held an Android workshop under the Google programme where the aim was to teach the participants and students the basics of Android , besides data structures and algorithms.

    The entire session was broken into 4-5 modules, each module covering a specific topic along with an assignment relevant to that topic.

    The first session included a rudimentary introduction to Java as a programming language and its comparison with C, the former being an OOP (Object Oriented Programming ) language. It also dealt with basics of Android , namely how to create a project, run the app, and build a simple user interface . The details of Android lifecycle with regards to starting , pausing and resuming , stopping and restarting and recreating an Activity was also emphasized along with intents.

Intro to Java

    The second session covered the following topics – hash set, hash map, arraylists and binary search along with some information on stacks and queues and games based on them.

    The third session focused more on advanced data structures, namely binary trees ( including binary search trees) , heaps , tries and priority queues and their implementation

    The fourth session dealt more exclusively on graphs and their implementation , specifically A* search and the puzzle game that required the use of the same.

    The fifth session involved a run-through of all the puzzles and apps made and fixing the bugs and code of any.

Scratch Workshop

Event Overview

On 17th October we had the most exciting and successful workshop of the semester – Scratch workshop.

For those who don’t know about scratch, its a programing language invented at MIT to help people start learning coding and do lots of creative stuff like make games, videos or just programs. The session focused majorly on game designing but we also explained other features as well and left it up to the participants to do what they thought was the best. After the 3 hour long workshop we opened the submissions cum feedback, and the best submissions out of the 40+ submission received were showcased on our FB page.

The best thing about this workshop was when you stumble across a guy at random and he recognizes you from the workshop, appreciated your work and asks for more workshops of the same manner ;)

3D printing Workshop

Event Overview

The 3D printing workshop was conducted by Kharagpur Open Source Society, in association with NSSC (National Students’ Space Challenge ) , powered by Srijan Creations, a Kolkata based 3D Printing institute.

3 D printing can be classified mainly into these following types:

1) FDM- FDM stands for Fused Deposition Modeling . It consists of an extruder- heating arrangement. The printing material or thermoplastic is fed into printer, where it is heated beyond it's melting point and is then deposited or extruded in layers to create a 3D printed object. This technique was demonstrated in this workshop. This is an example of additive manufacturing, and amongst 3D printing processes, it is the cheapest but time-consuming. In fact the greatest expense incurred in FDM manufacturing is the time taken – the cost of FDM printing is approximately Rs 400 per hour, while the raw material comes at Rs 2 per gram. The printer (FAB X), having a single extruder starts from the 20 k range . Those having dual extruders, one for the support material and the other for the finish product , are in the range of 1-1.5 lacs.
2) SLA – SLA stands for StereoLithoGraphy. It is an additive method like FDM , but differs in the technique used. It employs a process called curing where in a curable photoploymer resin is hardened by applying a focused light or UV light or laser light. In case of SLA printers, the lasers draw the layers, while in DLP (Digital Light Processing ) , an entire layer (a 2 –dimensional plane) of the model is projected at once in the resin bath. Consequently the DLP models are faster than the laser SLA ones. They are more reliable but easier to maintain.
The standard filament rolls used in FDM are thermoplastic in nature , though most are also blended with nylon, wood, ceramics ,metals, carbon fibres ,etc . They can vary in color and texture, unlike in SLA , which has a pre-determined choice of resin, and is comparatively more expensive.

The applicability of 3D printing is immense. It finds its use in engineering rapid prototypes in several sectors :
1) Medical – 3D printing can be used to create prosthetic and implantable body parts which require a high degree of precision and smooth finish.
2) Architecture – It is a relatively fast and inexpensive way of creating primitive models. In fact China employs 3D printing in creating actual houses and buildings.
3) Fashion- Jewellery, watches, ornaments, miniature statues and similar such objects can be designed on paper and then recreated as working models. They can easily pass off as those made in factories. Thus it has a huge commercial market, as anyone can make a 3 dimensional replica of their treasured moment or person, like wedding photographs or the match-box cars.
4) It also has a major RND aspect in the field of developing materials that can be used for printing.

Flipkart Hackathon

Event Overview

The very first event which was organised by KOSS, after its inception was an overnight Hackathon , in association with Flipkart.
     Participants, in groups of three( or less), were required to make an Android app, on spot. The central theme of the application pertained to India. Credit was awarded for ingenuity of the idea and practicality of the app , rather than its design or beauty. The competition commenced with an introductory seminar from the hosts, explaining the rules and regulations. This was followed by an extensive coding marathon that lasted way beyond the wee hours of the next morning and was punctured only by the desire to snack to keep the neurons firing.
The best 10 participating groups were given the unique opportunity of getting an internship or a PPO ,based on their current year, in Flipkart besides goodies and certificates.
After two tiers of gruelling evaluations, these three teams topped the list.
Team 3Gth - Vivek Aithal, Mihir Rege, Kunal Jain
Team Mother Kumar - Vikrant Varma, Amal Narayanan, Sreejith PP
Team Hackgp - G Surya Teja, Srinath Mandava, Dharmana Prudhvi

The hackathon was followed by an interactive session between the CTO of Flipkart , Amod Malviya, also an alumni from IIT KGP and the present batch, regarding placements and career opportunities. KOSS is greatly indebted to Flipkart and their team for their invaluable assistance. However, in retrospect it would not have been possible to successfully pull off this event had it not been for the dedication and guidance of our beloved seniors and mentors, Gaurav Jain and Smruti Ranjan Swain. All in all, it was a wonderful experience.
